Welcome to my personal workspace for tracking the development of Moneypenny, and the broader ideas behind Clear Thought Labs.

In April 2025, I published an article on Substack—As We May Yet Think—about how AI applications might serve as thought partners to help human users think more clearly and rationally. Two months later, a friend and I launched Clear Thought Labs to build a first version of such a system.

It turned out to be far more challenging than I expected. The information load alone was immense: schedules, decisions, design pivots, technical reading, new concepts like RAG and MCP, insights from AI research blogs . . . all of it accumulating faster than I could track or process. It became clear that I needed help managing not just the work, but the information and thinking surrounding the work.

I imagined an AI executive assistant—Moneypenny—named after the iconic Miss Moneypenny (Lois Maxwell in the early Bond films). Moneypenny would help manage information, organize ideas, and serve as a companion in learning the technologies behind our larger project.

The more I explored the concept, the more I realized I wanted two versions of Moneypenny:

  • a personal version, to help in handle my own life and intellectual workflow, and
  • a CTL version, to help in managing the development of products and the running of the company.

This site serves two purposes:

  1. A public window into the thinking, decisions, and experiments behind the project.
  2. A working log for architecture notes, design considerations, and ongoing development.

Over time, this will grow into a narrative record of the work — part journal, part technical notebook, part story.

A First Look at Memory

A key to Moneypenny is memory management. An AI assistant is only as useful as what it remembers, what it forgets, and how gracefully it moves between the two. Before diving into memory itself, I want to distinguish between a program and a project. A program is an ongoing activity that coordinates multiple efforts in pursuit of a larger, strategic aim. It may not have a fixed end date. Its outputs are capabilities rather than one-off deliverables. Success means: “Did we achieve the long-term benefits we intended?” A project, by contrast, is aimed at producing a specific outcome. ...

The Origins of Moneypenny

The original Moneypenny was meant to be an AI executive assistant to help manage the large amount of information associated with Cletho development. The personal Moneypenny I am developing here is meant to help me manage my own life and the my goals and objectives within it. The initial specific functions of the application are to assist in: Managing my calendar (which may include connections to other calendars). Maintaining a “To Do” list of things I want or need to do, and sending reminders for specified items. Developing and managing project plans. Keeping a time stamped, searchable journal of specified things I do over time. Keeping an time stamped, searchable log of ideas I come up with. Recording important decisions, with rationale and context, in an time stamped, searchable log. Maintaining an “Open Loops” list of things other people are responsible for doing, such as returning lent items, doing things they said they’d do, etc. Indexing digital artifacts, to wit, keep a record of where media articles, key discussion topics, document summaries, etc. can be found. Holding and publishing long-term reminders, e.g., such-and-such a subscription is expiring next month, Delaware taxes are due in a month, etc. Performing topical research on subjects of interest. Summarizing documents and identifying key points.
